What is Pomodoro?
The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The technique uses a timer to break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. These intervals are known as "Pomodoros," the plural in English of the Italian word Pomodoro (tomato), after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Cirillo used as a university student.

Here's a basic outline of how the Pomodoro Technique works:
  1. Choose a Task: Decide on the task you want to work on.
  2. Set the Timer: Set a timer for 25 minutes (one Pomodoro).
  3. Work on the Task: Work on the task until the timer rings.
  4. Take a Short Break: Take a short break, around 5 minutes.

Repeat: Repeat the process. After completing four Pomodoros, take a longer break, typically 15-30 minutes.

The idea behind the Pomodoro Technique is to break work into focused, manageable intervals, which can help improve concentration and reduce the impact of internal and external interruptions. The short breaks provide an opportunity for rest and recovery.

While the technique was initially developed for personal productivity, many people find it adaptable to various situations, including studying, writing, programming, or any task that requires focused attention. There are also numerous Pomodoro apps and timers available to help individuals implement this technique.
